Grilled Chicken with Spicy Thai Sauce   


Mary Abbott Hess, past-president of the American Dietetic Association and cook par excellence, submitted this recipe. She says the spicy dipping sacue is also good with broiled shrimp or scallops and is good over rice, couscous, or bulgur.
I tried making the sauce with less sugar, but the taste of vinegar was too strong. I then added the full 1/2 cup and found it just right. The few extra carbs are worth the taste.
 Yields: 8 Servings

Ingredients

8 Boneless, skinless chicken breast halves (about 6 ounces each)
1 cup Red bell pepper chopped
1 cup Vinegar
1/2 cup Sugar
1 teaspoon Crushed red hot pepper flakes
Optional:
1/2 cup Lemon Juice
4 cloves Garlic halves
1 teaspoon Salt


 
Instructions
 1. Optional: Marinate the chicken in lemon juice for several hours.
2. In a blender or food processor puree the red bell pepper and vinegar. Pour this mixture into a small saucepan. Add the sugar and bell pepper flakes and simmer the sauce for about 10 minutes. Remove the pan from the heat and let the sauce cool.
3. Drain the chicken, rub it with garlic, sprinkle with salt if desired.
4. Broil the chicken for about 5-7 minutes per side or until done.
5. Serve with individual small bowls of dipping sauce.

Note:Original Recipe was doubled
